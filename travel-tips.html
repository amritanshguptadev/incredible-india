Travel Smart in India

Essential knowledge for your journey through Bharat

Traveller's Handbook

Everything You Need to Know

Packing Essentials

  • Cotton clothes for humid coasts; woolen layers for the Himalayas
  • Comfortable walking shoes AND sandals — you'll need both
  • First aid kit: ORS sachets, antidiarrheal, antihistamine tablets
  • Power bank (10,000 mAh minimum) — power cuts are common
  • Physical photocopies of passport, visa, and train tickets
  • Mosquito repellent and sunscreen SPF 50+
  • Lightweight raincoat — monsoon reaches everywhere, even the desert

Budget Travel

  • Indian Railways is the cheapest intercity option — book 60 days early on IRCTC
  • Sleeper class trains cost under ₹500 for 12-hour journeys
  • Eat at local dhabas for ₹60–120 per meal — tastier than tourist restaurants
  • State buses (GSRTC, KSRTC) are cheap and cover all major routes
  • Book mid-range hotels on MakeMyTrip for the best local rates
  • Walk 2 streets away from tourist landmarks — prices halve immediately

Safety Tips

  • Use only prepaid government taxis from airports — avoid unlicensed touts
  • Use Ola or Uber in cities; always share your ride details with someone
  • Common scams: "closed monument" redirect, gem export schemes, fake guides near Taj Mahal
  • Keep both digital and physical copies of all important documents
  • Leave your passport in the hotel safe — don't carry it in a bag
  • Trust your instincts — if something feels wrong, walk away calmly

Cultural Etiquette

  • Remove footwear before entering all temples, mosques, gurudwaras, and homes
  • Cover shoulders and knees in religious sites — scarves are available at most entrances
  • Greet with Namaste — it is universally appropriate and deeply respected
  • Always ask permission clearly before photographing local people
  • Avoid public displays of affection in smaller towns and religious areas
  • Accept food and gifts with your right hand — the left is considered unclean in tradition

Food Guide

  • Start mild — your stomach needs 3 days to adjust to Indian spices
  • "Veg" in India means no meat, eggs, or fish — strictly plant-based
  • Always drink bottled or filtered water — never tap water anywhere
  • Street food is mostly safe from vendors with high turnover and fresh oil
  • Thali meals give maximum variety for minimum price — order one daily
  • Must-try nationwide: dosa, biryani, chaat, paneer butter masala, kulfi, lassi

Train Travel Guide

  • Book on the IRCTC app or website — create your account at least one week early
  • Class guide: 3A (3-tier AC) is comfortable and affordable; 2A is premium; Sleeper is budget
  • Tatkal quota opens 24 hours before departure for last-minute bookings
  • Download the NTES app for live train tracking across all routes
  • Each major station has a Tourist Quota — visit the Station Manager if trains show full online
  • Carry snacks for long journeys — pantry car quality is unpredictable
