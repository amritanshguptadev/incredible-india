Explore India's Wonders

Detailed guides to India's most extraordinary destinations

JAIPUR
Rajasthan

Jaipur

The Pink City

Best Time
October – March
Climate
Arid, Hot Summers

Top Attractions

  • Amber Fort — hilltop Rajput palace with dazzling mirror halls
  • Hawa Mahal — the Palace of Winds, graced with 953 windows
  • City Palace — royal complex still home to the royal family
  • Jantar Mantar — UNESCO World Heritage astronomical observatory

Must Eat

Dal Baati Churma Ghewar Laal Maas Pyaaz Kachori

Jaipur was founded in 1727 by Maharaja Jai Singh II — one of India's first planned cities. The city was painted pink in 1876 to welcome Prince Albert, and the colour has never left. Walk its bazaars at dusk and the sandstone glows the colour of sunset.

GOA
Goa

Goa

The Golden Coast

Best Time
November – February
Climate
Tropical, Humid

Top Attractions

  • Baga & Palolem Beaches — sun-drenched shores for every mood
  • Basilica of Bom Jesus — 400-year-old UNESCO baroque church
  • Dudhsagar Falls — four-tiered milky cascade deep in the jungle
  • Anjuna Flea Market — bohemian Sunday market since the 1960s

Must Eat

Fish Curry Rice Bebinca Prawn Balchão Xacuti

For 450 years a Portuguese colony, Goa blends Latin flair with Indian soul. Whitewashed churches stand beside Hindu temples. The air smells of sea salt, cashew feni, and jasmine — an intoxicating mix that keeps people coming back year after year.

VARANASI
Uttar Pradesh

Varanasi

The Eternal City

Best Time
October – March
Climate
Hot, Humid Monsoon

Top Attractions

  • Dashashwamedh Ghat — spectacular nightly Ganga Aarti ceremony
  • Manikarnika Ghat — the sacred cremation ghat burning for 3,500 years
  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple — one of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines
  • Sarnath — where the Buddha delivered his first sermon

Must Eat

Banarasi Paan Kachori Sabzi Malaiyo Thandai

Varanasi is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities on Earth — older than history, older than tradition. Mark Twain called it "older than history, older than tradition, older even than legend." At dawn, a boat on the Ganga will change how you see life and death forever.

KERALA
Kerala

Kerala

God's Own Country

Best Time
September – March
Climate
Tropical, Lush

Top Attractions

  • Alleppey Backwaters — rice-boat cruises through emerald waterways
  • Munnar Tea Gardens — rolling hills carpeted in green at 1,700m
  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary — elephants and tigers in misty jungle
  • Kovalam Beach — crescent bays framed by red laterite cliffs

Must Eat

Sadya on Banana Leaf Appam & Stew Kerala Fish Curry Puttu & Kadala

Kerala is India distilled to its gentlest essence — a narrow strip of land between the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea, threading 44 rivers and 900 km of backwaters. Ayurveda was born here. So was Kathakali dance. So was the idea that paradise might actually exist.

LADAKH
Ladakh

Leh-Ladakh

The Last Frontier

Best Time
June – September
Climate
Cold Desert, High Altitude

Top Attractions

  • Pangong Lake — electric-blue lake at 4,350m straddling India & China
  • Nubra Valley — sand dunes and double-humped Bactrian camels
  • Thiksey Monastery — 12-storey gompa rising like a Tibetan Potala
  • Khardung La Pass — one of the world's highest motorable roads at 5,359m

Must Eat

Thukpa Momos Skyu Butter Tea

Ladakh sits at the roof of the world where India, Pakistan, China and Tibet converge. The air is thin, the sky impossibly blue, and every mountain pass is draped in prayer flags. It is the kind of place that makes you feel simultaneously very small and very alive.

RISHIKESH
Uttarakhand

Rishikesh

The Yoga Capital of the World

Best Time
Sep–Nov, Feb–May
Climate
Subtropical, Pleasant

Top Attractions

  • Laxman Jhula — iconic iron suspension bridge over the Ganga
  • Triveni Ghat — mesmerising evening Aarti on the sacred river banks
  • Beatles Ashram — where Lennon & McCartney composed the White Album
  • White Water Rafting — thrilling 16–35 km runs on the holy Ganga

Must Eat

Chole Bhature Lassi Aloo Puri Rhododendron Juice

Rishikesh sits at the precise point where the Ganga escapes the Himalayas and enters the plains. In 1968, the Beatles visited Maharishi Mahesh Yogi here and composed most of the White Album in meditation. The world discovered yoga through Rishikesh — and Rishikesh discovered something of the world too.

HAMPI
Karnataka

Hampi

Ruins of a Lost Empire

Best Time
October – February
Climate
Dry, Warm Winters

Top Attractions

  • Virupaksha Temple — continuously active since the 7th century
  • Stone Chariot at Vittala Temple — the iconic UNESCO symbol of Hampi
  • Hemakuta Hill — hilltop temples bathed in molten sunset light
  • Matanga Hill — panoramic sunrise over boulders and banana groves

Must Eat

Jolada Rotti Obbattu Benne Dosa

Hampi was once the second-largest city on Earth and the richest in the world — capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, glittering with diamonds and spice. It was razed in 1565 and abandoned for 500 years, its ruins left frozen among volcanic boulders like a civilisation paused mid-breath.

KUTCH
Gujarat

Rann of Kutch

The Great White Desert

Best Time
Nov – Feb (Rann Utsav)
Climate
Arid, Extreme Seasons

Top Attractions

  • White Rann — moonlit walks across the world's largest salt flat
  • Kala Dungar (Black Hill) — highest point in Kutch, legendary sunset
  • Dholavira — magnificent Indus Valley civilisation ruins (UNESCO)
  • Flamingo City — thousands of flamingos nesting in the marshes

Must Eat

Dabeli Kutchi Khichdi Rotlo with Ringna Chikki

After monsoon waters recede, the Rann transforms into the world's largest salt flat — 7,500 sq km of blinding white silence. Under a full moon, the horizon disappears entirely and you feel suspended between the earth and sky. The Rann Utsav festival brings this lunar landscape to life each winter.

ANDAMAN
Andaman & Nicobar

Andaman Islands

Emerald Seas at World's Edge

Best Time
October – May
Climate
Tropical Island, Humid

Top Attractions

  • Radhanagar Beach — consistently ranked among Asia's finest shores
  • Cellular Jail — colonial-era prison that held India's freedom fighters
  • Havelock Island — world-class snorkelling in crystalline waters
  • North Bay — vibrant coral reefs and underwater walking trails

Must Eat

Grilled Barracuda Coconut Prawn Curry Jackfruit Curry Fresh Coconut Water

The British used the Andamans as a penal colony to imprison Indian independence fighters. The Cellular Jail held 696 solitary cells — today it stands as a monument to the price of freedom, its walls still echoing with the names of those who refused to break.

MEGHALAYA
Meghalaya

Meghalaya

The Abode of Clouds

Best Time
September – May
Climate
Cool, Extremely Wet

Top Attractions

  • Living Root Bridges — Khasi-trained rubber tree roots spanning rivers
  • Dawki River — kayak on glass-clear water above the riverbed below
  • Nohkalikai Falls — India's tallest plunge waterfall at 340m
  • Mawsynram — the wettest inhabited place on the planet

Must Eat

Jadoh Tungrymbai Dohneiiong Pukhlein Rice Cake

The Khasi people of Meghalaya began guiding rubber tree roots across rivers 500 years ago — training living wood to bridge impossible gorges. These bridges grow stronger each decade rather than weaker. In a world obsessed with building, Meghalaya teaches us that some things are worth growing slowly.
